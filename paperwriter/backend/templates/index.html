Frequently Asked Questions

Is my research data secure and private under DPDP?
Absolutely. Under the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 of India, we process your personal data and documents strictly based on your explicit consent. Your research drafts are private, encrypted in transit, and are never used to train machine learning models.
How do I export to a LaTeX ZIP project?
Open any paper, click the "Export" button on the bottom sidebar, and select "Export LaTeX Project (ZIP)". This packages your `.tex` source file, BibTeX `.bib` bibliography database, all uploaded figures, and the IEEE class style file into a single structured archive.
Why does local PDF compilation fail?
Local PDF compilation requires a LaTeX distribution (like TeX Live or MiKTeX) installed on your operating system. If PaperWriter cannot locate a local LaTeX engine, it automatically routes the compilation request to our secure cloud compiler so your exports never fail.
How do I manage bibliography citations (BibTeX)?
Open the Citations sidebar from the left panel. You can fetch citation entries automatically by typing a DOI or paste your raw BibTeX entries directly. To cite a reference in your text, insert the citation label (e.g. `\cite{key}`).
Can I collaborate with co-authors?
Yes. Open the "Authors" dialog from the sidebar to manage your co-authors in IEEE ordinal order. All author metadata (names, departments, email, and affiliations) will be dynamically formatted on the compiled title block.
How do I get more export credits?
Click the "Upgrade" button in the navigation header. You can purchase additional credits securely via Razorpay or redeem a promotional code provided by your institution or conference.

Privacy Policy & DPDP Compliance

×

1. Introduction

Welcome to PaperWriter. We are deeply committed to protecting your personal data and ensuring compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 of India, along with standard global data privacy regulations (such as GDPR). This policy describes what data we collect, how it is processed, and your rights as a Data Principal.

2. Terminology under DPDP Act

• Data Principal (You): The individual who owns and creates the personal data.
• Data Fiduciary (PaperWriter): The entity that determines the purpose and means of processing personal data.

3. Data Collection and Sources

We only collect data that is strictly necessary for providing the services of the Scholarly Workspace. This includes:

• Account Credentials: Email address and name retrieved via secure Google Sign-In.
• User-Generated Content: Your paper drafts, sections, author names, references, uploaded figures, and mathematical tables.
• Technical Data: Anonymized usage logs, IP addresses, session cookies required to persist login states, and error reports.

4. Purpose and Legal Basis of Processing

We process your data strictly based on your explicit consent, which is recorded upon your initial registration. The processing is carried out for the following purposes:

• Rendering and compiling LaTeX drafts to IEEE-formatted PDFs or ZIP archives.
• Auto-saving drafts to our servers so you can sync and access your papers across devices.
• Translating descriptions and scans into mathematical code using the Google Gemini API (done securely, without storing drafts for model training).
• Processing optional transaction callbacks through Razorpay to unlock extra download credits.

Note: We do NOT sell, lease, or use your manuscripts to train artificial intelligence models without your explicit, separate consent.

5. Rights of the Data Principal

In accordance with the DPDP Act, 2023, you hold the following rights:

• Right to Access: You can view all documents, authors, and metadata stored on your profile at any time in your dashboard.
• Right to Correction & Completion: You can edit any document titles, co-authors, bibliography references, or metadata fields instantly.
• Right to Withdraw Consent: You can toggle or withdraw consent settings within your Profile panel.
• Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): You have the absolute right to erase all data. Clicking "Erase My Data" in your Profile instantly and permanently deletes your account, user profile, and all associated manuscripts from our database and disk.

6. Security and Data Retention

Your data is stored in secure database clusters and is encrypted in transit using SSL/TLS. We retain your personal data only as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, all personal details and document content are deleted immediately without recovery options.

7. Third-Party Integrations

To offer advanced workspace features, we interact with the following trusted third parties:

• Google LLC: Used for secure single-sign-on (SSO) authentication.
• Razorpay Software Pvt. Ltd.: Secure processing of payments for credit top-ups.
• Google Gemini API: Used exclusively to perform mathematical translations and formatting adjustments when you invoke AI commands.

8. Grievance Redressal and Contact

If you have any questions, concerns, or grievances regarding our data processing practices, or wish to report an issue to our Data Protection Officer, you can submit an inquiry via the Feedback Form on the dashboard or email us directly at dpo@paperwriter.app.
